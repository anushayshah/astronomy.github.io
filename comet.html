What is Comets?

Comets are frozen leftovers from the formation of the solar system composed of dust, rock, and ices. They range from a few miles to tens of miles wide, but as they orbit closer to the Sun, they heat up and spew gases and dust into a glowing head that can be larger than a planet. This material forms a tail that stretches millions of miles.

Comet Tempel
1 Impact

Deep Impact sent an impactor into Tempel 1,
becoming the first spacecraft to eject material from
the surface of a comet.Changes in the surface of
Tempel 1 were obscured by all the material ejected
during and after the collision.
The impact site was then re-imaged by a
second mission, Stardust NExT, in 2011.


Comet Shoemaker-Levy
9 Fragments (1994)

The impacts proved to be impressive: The fragments – some 21 in
all – plunged into Jupiter's atmosphere over the course of six days.
At impact, they were traveling at a speed of about 37 miles/second
(60 km/second), heating the atmosphere to at least 53,000 degrees
Fahrenheit or 30,000 degrees Celsius.
