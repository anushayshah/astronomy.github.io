What is Astronomy?

Astronomy is the scientific study of celestial objects such as stars, planets, galaxies, and the universe as a whole. It encompasses various disciplines, including observational and theoretical astronomy.

How Does a Telescope Work?

Telescopes use lenses or mirrors to gather and focus light from distant objects. This concentrated light enables astronomers to observe celestial bodies with greater detail and clarity.

What is the Big Bang Theory?

The Big Bang Theory is the prevailing explanation for the origin of the universe. It suggests that the universe began as an infinitely hot and dense point and has been expanding and cooling ever since.

How Do Stars Form?

Stars form from dense clouds of gas and dust called nebulae. Gravity causes these clouds to collapse, leading to the ignition of nuclear fusion and the birth of a new star.

What Are Black Holes?

Black holes are extremely dense regions in space where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape. They are formed from the remnants of massive stars after a supernova explosion

What Causes Eclipses?

Solar eclipses occur when the Moon passes between the Earth and the Sun, blocking the Sun's light. Lunar eclipses occur when the Earth passes between the Sun and the Moon, casting a shadow on the Moon

How Do Planets Orbit the Sun?

Planets orbit the Sun due to the gravitational force between them and the Sun. This force keeps them in a stable elliptical path around the Sun.

What Are Exoplanets?

Exoplanets, or extrasolar planets, are planets that orbit stars outside our solar system. Their discovery has expanded our understanding of planetary systems.

Can I See Planets with the Naked Eye?

Yes, several planets, including Venus, Mars, Jupiter, and Saturn, are visible to the naked eye from Earth at various times of the year
